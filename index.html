FLAGSHIP Case 01 · AI Agent Product Design

EVA — An AI Agent for Knowledge Work

A multi-module AI agent that turns meetings, messages, email, tasks, and travel into structured drafts the user can confirm, edit, or reject. Designed around a knowledge-graph substrate and a perception → analysis → decision → action loop.

Role

Senior Product Designer (lead) — product definition, AI capability mapping, interaction design, and human-in-the-loop framework.

Scope

Phase 2 product design. Five AI modules: Meeting, IM, Email, Event-Task, and Travel — unified by a personal & organizational knowledge graph.

Design Stance

Active assistance over passive Q&A. The agent always produces drafts — never silent commits. Every suggestion is confirmable, editable, reversible.

Problem

Knowledge workers don't lack tools — they lack a layer above the tools.

Meetings, IM, email, tasks, and travel each have a category leader, but the cognitive load lives in the seams between them. Action items get lost between a Zoom call and a chat thread. Email drafts repeat decisions already made. Reminders fire on the wrong rhythm. Existing assistants answer questions on demand — useful, but reactive. The opportunity is to design an agent that actively maintains context across surfaces, while keeping the human firmly in control of every commit.

Design Challenge

How might we design an AI agent that proactively reduces cognitive load across work surfaces — without ever making the user feel out of control?

The product bet was active rather than passive AI. The design challenge was making that bet feel safe. That meant turning every model output into a draft, every suggestion into a card, every decision into something the user could edit, ignore, or undo — without diluting the agent's usefulness in the process.

"AI proposes, human disposes" — every artefact ships with confirm, edit, ignore, and reversibility built into the interface.

Target Users

Business and knowledge workers running high-frequency meetings, multi-channel communication, and concurrent project responsibilities.

Primary users are senior individual contributors and managers who context-switch many times per day, often across time zones and stakeholders. They are tool-fluent but time-constrained. They are willing to delegate drafting, but not decisions.

Frequent meetings Multi-channel comms Cross-timezone Decision authority Tool-fluent, time-poor

Human-in-the-Loop Principles

Five principles the entire product is bound to.

P1

Drafts, never commits

Every model output lands as an editable draft — meeting agenda, task card, email body, travel plan. Nothing is finalized without a confirm.

P2

Transparent reasoning

Each suggestion shows a short "why" string: "Eng Lead — based on past Q1–Q3 reviews." Users can override and the system learns.

P3

Reversibility

Created items remain editable; reminders are dismissible; drafts can be discarded; importance labels can be re-graded.

P4

Source preservation

Original messages, emails, files, and meeting clips remain one click away from every generated artefact.

P5

Adjustable behaviour

Reminder cadence, quiet hours, importance thresholds, and notification surfaces are user-configurable defaults — not hidden settings.

Adaptive Reminder · Conflict Handling

Conflicts and reminders surface as offers, not demands. The preferred option is highlighted with reasoning; alternatives are equally first-class. Quiet hours and thresholds are part of the contract.

Edge Cases & Tradeoffs

What the agent does when it doesn't know — and what it never does.

E1

Ambiguous input

"No relevant context found" + manual fallback. The agent does not guess — it shows what it considered and asks the user to choose.

E2

Topic drift in meetings

Soft warning icon + "Guide back to topic" prompt the host can accept or dismiss. Never auto-redirects the conversation.

E3

Schedule conflicts

Conflicts surface before commit, with ranked alternatives that include a "do nothing — keep both" option to preserve user agency.

E4

Importance misgrading

Importance labels are colour-coded but always overrideable. The model treats user overrides as the ground truth.

E5

Notification fatigue

Custom cadence (1h / 4h / daily), quiet hours, and a "hold non-urgent" rule prevent the agent from becoming noise.

E6

Pre-meeting briefing overload

Open tradeoff: dense context can backfire. Briefings collapse to one card by default; deeper detail is opt-in.

MVP Scope

Phase 2 prioritises ROI over coverage.

Meeting Assist ships first as the highest-ROI surface and the most demanding test of the human-in-the-loop framework. IM and Email follow. Event-Task is sequenced as the integration layer once the upstream modules are stable. Travel Assist is deprioritised — high complexity, lower frequency. The knowledge graph runs through every release and compounds with usage.

Transferable Design Patterns

Patterns that move from a consumer-leaning agent into enterprise AI surfaces.

  • Card-based suggestion pattern — every AI output is a card the user can confirm, edit, or reject. Directly transferable to AI features inside operational dashboards, intake forms, and review workflows.
  • Reasoning strings — short "why this" explanations on every recommendation. The cheapest, highest-leverage trust mechanism in AI UI.
  • Floating-window pattern — non-modal, draggable AI surfaces that preserve user context inside third-party tools. Transferable to enterprise plug-ins and side-panels.
  • One confirmation pattern across modules — meeting agendas, task cards, email drafts, travel plans all share the same confirm / edit / ignore / undo surface. Lowers the cognitive cost of trusting the agent.
  • Knowledge graph as substrate — personalisation compounds across modules without explicit configuration. Pattern for enterprise platforms where users won't configure preferences but will benefit from learned behaviour.
CASE 02 Decision-Support Data Product

Threat Intelligence Decision-Support Dashboard

A tiered decision-support data product for sensitive intelligence — KPI governance, severity tiering, and permission-aware visibility, with controlled handoff to external response systems.

Role

Product Manager — dashboard logic, KPI definition and governance, PRD structuring, data-assumption alignment, and client-facing metric explanation.

Design focus

The product's value comes from KPI discipline. Every indicator is defined as a governed artefact — formula, inputs, time window, severity threshold, assumptions. Visibility is layered: global → regional → entity → multi-entity investigation, with role and organisation scope deciding what is visible at each tier.

  • Tiered drill-down (global → regional → entity) with one consistent filter and time-window model across tiers
  • Composite scoring driving Critical / Elevated / Normal severity tiers with shared visual language
  • Role × organisation visibility model — what data shows, what filters allow, what export permits
  • Read-only intelligence with controlled handoff to external response systems
  • Client-facing metric explanation as a first-class product output
CASE 03 Public-Sector Platform Projects

Drone Regulation & Monitoring Platforms

A series of related public-sector platform projects spanning audience-tailored service journeys, regulator-facing real-time monitoring, and a governance / RBAC foundation — with AI add-ons positioned as supporting capabilities under human-in-the-loop review.

Role

Product Lead — structured product logic across three connected areas, with shared lifecycle, role, and data-scope considerations.

Design focus

The three areas share one application engine but serve very different audiences. The design challenge was hiding multi-entity complexity behind a single predictable public-facing journey, while giving regulators an operational view with map → statistics → drill-down consistency. AI add-ons (intake, review, self-service) sit on top of this foundation, never replacing the human review step.

  • Six audience portals (individual, business, manufacturer, distributor, point of sale, government partner) sharing one approval engine
  • Operational monitoring: map → statistic cards → pop-up detail → drill-down table, with consistent filter logic
  • Governance: Module + Action + Data Scope + Audit Requirement permission model
  • AI add-ons (intake, review, self-service) explicitly scoped under human-in-the-loop checkpoints
  • Phase 1 deliverability without closing the door on future scale
CASE 04 Government Workflow Platform

Aviation Request & Operations Platform

A workflow and governance platform pairing a mobile requester app with a web operations console — bilingual EN / AR, four-role RBAC, three-channel notifications, and a phased delivery plan structured around foundation, lifecycle, and monitoring.

Role

Acting Client Owner — requirement clarification, workflow structuring, PRD authoring, stakeholder alignment, UAT support, and delivery coordination.

Design focus

Two surfaces — a mobile requester app and a web operations console — had to behave as one product. Every capability was mapped across both, with a state machine (Draft → Under Review → Confirmed → Completed, with Cancelled / Unable as side states) shared between them. Notifications were modelled as a three-channel system tied to lifecycle stages. Bilingual EN / AR with RTL was treated as a product-layer concern, not a localisation pass.

  • Lifecycle states with clear ownership and side branches
  • Four-role × multi-module RBAC matrix across mobile + web
  • Three-channel notifications (in-app · push · SMS) mapped to lifecycle stages
  • Bilingual EN / AR with RTL handled at the product layer
  • Phased delivery (foundation · lifecycle · monitoring) carried into UAT and production
Design Approach

How I work — especially when AI is part of the product.

My approach is structured around five stages. Each one produces outputs that reduce ambiguity for the next.

Step 01

Perceive

Understand business context, user roles, operational pain points, data sources, and where AI can add value — and where it shouldn't.

Step 02

Structure

Convert fragmented needs into product scope: workflow, data logic, permissions, AI capability boundaries, and phased delivery.

Step 03

Design

PRDs, user flows, dashboard structures, RBAC models, AI behaviour specs, and prototypes for both happy paths and edge cases.

Step 04

Align

Coordinate clients, technical leads, vendors, and management to clarify assumptions, surface tradeoffs, and reduce interpretation gaps.

Step 05

Deliver

UAT support, issue clarification, acceptance alignment, and production readiness. Design owned end-to-end, not handed off.
